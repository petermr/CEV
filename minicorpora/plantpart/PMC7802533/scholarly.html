Abstract
Background

The chemical composition of Handal (Citrullus colocynthis L.) seed oil cultivated in Jordan deserts was characterized, and its bioactivity was evaluated.

Methods

The oil was extracted from the grinded seeds in 500Â ml Soxhlet extractor for 24Â hr using nâ€�hexane, and the recovered fatty acids were methylated with methanolicâ€�HCL. The fatty acid methyl esters (FAMEs) composition was analyzed using GCâ€�MS and GCâ€�FID. The anticancer activity associated with the oil was assessed against colon cancer cell lines (Cacoâ€�2 and HCTâ€�116) and compared to its cytotoxicity on the human skin fibroblast. Results

The results demonstrated that fatty acid composition of Citrullus colocynthis seed oil chiefly contains Linoleic acid, denoted as C18:2n6 (75%), followed by Palmitic acid C16:0 (8%), Stearic acid C18:0 (5%), and Oleic acid C18:1n9 (9%). It is demonstrated as an excellent source of essential fatty acids omegaâ€�6 (e.g., Linoleic acid), whereas omegaâ€�3 (e.g., Î±â€�Linolenic acid) and hydroxy polyunsaturated fatty acids are found at small level. Interestingly, the oil exhibited reasonable anticancer effects against colorectal cancer cell lines with IC50 values varying between 4 and 7Â mg/ml. The correlation test revealed a relationship between the fatty acid composition and the effectiveness on treatments.

Conclusions

Handal plant from Jordan appears to have very high level of Linoleic acid compared to other oils measured in different geographic locations and that there appears to be some anticancer activities associated with the fatty acid content of Handal seed oil.

CONCLUSIONS

The major fatty acids present in Handal seed oil from the desert of Jordan are Linoleic acid 74.8Â Â±Â 0.1 (%), Palmitic acid 8.35Â Â±Â 0.03 (%), Stearic acid 5.36Â Â±Â 0.06 (%), and Oleic acid 9.04Â Â±Â 0.09 (%), while Omega 3â€� and hydroxy polyunsaturated fatty acids are found at less than 1%. The content of linoleic oil in this study is higher than that of Handel oil from other regions. The results of the present study concluded that cancer cell viability was considerably affected after 48Â hr exposure relative to the human fibroblasts upon treatment with the examined oil, suggesting a reasonable anticancer activity and safety profile against the human skin fibroblasts. The principal component analysis (PCA) suggests a potential relationship between the high omegaâ€�6 fatty acid in natural Handal seed oil and the anticancer activities reported against the studied cancer cell lines.

Overall, these findings indicate that Handal plant from Jordan has very high level of linoleic acid compared to other oils measured in different geographic locations and that there appears to be some anticancer activities associated with the fatty acid profile of Handel seed oil. Further studies using isolated constituents instead of the whole extract should be carried out to better understand the relationship between Handel seed oil composition and potential anticancer activity.
